Dalhousie U
Hospitality Brand System

Project Overview

Dalhousie University needed a unified brand system to connect five distinct dining halls and multiple cafes across campus. The challenge was creating individual identities for each location while maintaining cohesive recognition under one master hospitality brand.

The solution uses a circle motif as the unifying design element, creating a flexible system that ties every dining location together. Each dining hall features its own circular icon that reflects its personality and student community, while cafes use bold divided circles differentiated by color from the university palette.

Marketing Programs

The brand system extends beyond visual identity to create engaging student programming. The Battle of the Houses competition brings residence halls together in culinary competitions, leveraging each dining hall's unique mascot and identity to build community engagement.

These marketing activations demonstrate how the brand system creates opportunities for student connection and campus culture. The flexible design language adapts across print materials, digital signage, and environmental graphics while maintaining consistent recognition.

Cafe Network

The cafe sub-brands create distinct personalities for specialized dining locations. Each cafe uses the divided circle system with signature colors to establish individual recognition while remaining clearly connected to Dalhousie Hospitality.

From Green Cafe's health-forward sustainability focus to Topio's pizza and gelato concept, each location communicates its unique offering through clear typography and color differentiation. The system ensures students can easily navigate campus dining options while building loyalty across the entire network.
