The Brief: Evolution, Not Revolution

"Modernize our logo while preserving 30+ years of brand equity."

Liberty Entertainment Group reached out for a brand identity refresh. Their existing logo—featuring classic serif typography with an integrated torch element—had served them well but felt dated against the contemporary hospitality landscape. The challenge was nuanced: evolve the visual identity to feel current and premium without losing the heritage and recognition they'd built across their Toronto venues.

Project Objectives:

  • • Modernize typography while maintaining luxury positioning
  • • Create flexible system for digital and physical applications
  • • Develop secondary lockups for diverse use cases
  • • Retain recognizable brand elements where possible

Key Constraints: Must work across 500,000+ sq ft of signage. Digital-first but print-ready. Appeal to both event planners and diners.

Process: Round 1 — Torch Integration & Typography Refinement

I began by exploring how to modernize the torch element while refining the typography. The initial concepts ranged from elegant and refined to bold and contemporary.

Concept V1: Featured thin, elegant typography with a modernized torch icon—maintaining the heritage element while refining it for contemporary use. The upright torch above the "I" in LIBERTY subtly reinforced the existing equity.

Concept V2: Took a bolder approach, using heavier type for stronger presence. This direction removed decorative elements to focus purely on typography—powerful and impactful, it positioned the brand as strong, confident, and modern.

Concept V3 (Recommended): Balanced elegance with boldness—retaining the torch icon while ensuring strong visibility. The balanced proportions made it adaptable across luxury and mainstream applications. This direction was positioned as the ideal evolution: modern enough for today's market while respecting 30+ years of brand heritage.

Stakeholder Feedback: Push Further

Client requested further modernization—less traditional, more contemporary.

While V3 represented a safe evolution, the client wanted to explore bolder territory. This feedback gave me permission to break away from the torch element entirely and investigate more unexpected directions. This kind of iterative refinement—where stakeholder feedback pushes the creative work into new territory—is where the most interesting solutions emerge.

Process: Round 2 — Exploring Bolder, Contemporary Directions

Armed with client feedback, I explored dramatically different visual territories:

Literal Hospitality Iconography: Explored martini glass and abstract ice cube forms—moving away from the torch entirely. These directions felt literal and direct, but risked being too on-the-nose for a brand managing such diverse venue types.

Typography-Focused Directions: Geometric accents—starburst symbols and modernist treatments. These concepts emphasized strong, contemporary typography with subtle decorative elements. Bold and clean, they projected strength and sophistication without relying on literal hospitality clichés.

This exploration phase demonstrated range and creative flexibility—showing the client how far we could push while maintaining brand integrity.

Custom Typography: Hand-Crafted Letterforms

Personal highlight: This custom typeface became my favorite from the project. The distinctive inline strokes within each letterform create an art deco-inspired elegance that feels both timeless and fresh. The vertical striations reference architectural details—columns, fluting, the grandeur of Liberty's historic venues like Casa Loma. Each character was carefully crafted to balance sophistication with readability at scale.

While this direction wasn't selected for final implementation, it represents the level of craft and custom typography work I bring to identity projects. The client appreciated the craftsmanship but ultimately felt it was too ornate for their modernization goals. This is the nature of iterative design—not every direction moves forward, but each exploration informs the final solution.

Final Direction: Modernized Heritage

The approved logo system—currently live across Liberty venues.

The final approved direction retained the torch icon—a key equity element—while modernizing the overall typography and proportions. The system includes a primary lockup and standalone icon for flexible application across digital and physical touchpoints.

Why This Direction Won:

  • • Preserved brand recognition through the torch element
  • • Updated typography feels contemporary without being trendy
  • • Flexible lockups work across diverse venue applications
  • • Scalable from favicon to building signage
